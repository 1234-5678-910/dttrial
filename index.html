Context & Situation

Black magic in India is more than folklore — it contributes to crime, fraud, violence, and psychological distress. Surveys show many people believe in its effects, often influenced by tradition and media, while court cases highlight growing legal concerns. Although belief remains common, many victims seek medical or psychological help, linking superstition with mental health.

Pain Points & Needs

Pain Points

  • 1. Blind belief in supernatural traditions and rituals
  • 2. Generational/community fear and social pressure
  • 3. Anxiety, stress, emotional insecurity and fear of curses
  • 4. Dependence on supernatural explanations during problems
  • 5. Misinterpreting coincidences due to confirmation bias
  • 6. Influence of horror/paranormal media and misinformation
  • 7. Lack of scientific awareness and critical thinking
  • 8. Emotional dependence on rituals and spiritual healers

Needs

  • 1. Scientific awareness, rational understanding, evidence-based thinking
  • 2. Critical thinking, logical reasoning, and psychology/cognitive bias education
  • 3. Mental health awareness, counseling, stress management, safe emotional support
  • 4. Open family/community discussions and supportive guidance networks
  • 5. Media literacy and awareness of misinformation/superstition

Root Causes (5 Whys)

P
Problem: Young adults experiencing supernatural distress have no safe, effective support system.
1
Why? Family, religion, and community traditions normalize supernatural beliefs without scientific grounding.
2
Why? Stress, anxiety, and fear of the unknown encourage supernatural explanations over psychological ones.
3
Why? Paranormal media and limited scientific understanding strengthen irrational beliefs.
4
Why? Community influence and spiritual healers reinforce belief and provide emotional comfort.
5
Root Cause: No culturally-sensitive platforms exist that distinguish legitimate practices from exploitation, explain psychological mechanisms accessibly, provide integrated support pathways, and create safe spaces for the "gray zone" of uncertainty.

How Might We...?

HMW #1

Provide 24/7 confidential counseling with grounding and culturally-sensitive support for students in distress?

HMW #2

Help users distinguish between legitimate cultural practitioners and those exploiting supernatural beliefs?

HMW #3

Use AI-based assessments to guide users toward suitable medical, cultural, or integrated support?

HMW #4

Build a transparent certification system for ethical cultural practitioners who respect mental health boundaries?
