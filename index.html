Systems Thinking

User / Protagonist

Primary Persona: "Akhil"

"I don't know what I believe anymore. It sits in this uncomfortable middle space."

  • • 20-year-old engineering student
  • • Science-minded, didn't believe in black magic
  • • Participated in ritual to "prove it was fake"
  • • Experienced 14-16 hours of psychological distress

Context & Situation

Research Scope

310 survey responses from university students (18-25 yrs) + 5 in-depth interviews.

Key Cultural Tension: Young Indians navigating family traditions, social media influence, AND scientific education—often without integrated frameworks to process unexplained experiences.

Evidence Base

Key Findings (n=310)

Belief Distribution:

Yes: 34.6% | No: 34.6% | Maybe: 30.7%

Mental Health Impact:

53.5% report "A Lot" or "Somewhat" affected

Pain Points & Needs

Combined Pain Points

  1. Blind belief in supernatural traditions and rituals
  2. Generational/community fear and social pressure
  3. Anxiety, stress, emotional insecurity and fear of curses
  4. Dependence on supernatural explanations during problems
  5. Misinterpreting coincidences due to confirmation bias
  6. Influence of horror/paranormal media and misinformation
  7. Lack of scientific awareness and critical thinking
  8. Emotional dependence on rituals and spiritual healers

Root Causes (5 Whys)

Problem: Young adults experiencing supernatural distress have no safe, effective support system
↓ Why #1: Cultural & Traditional Influence
↓ Why #2: Fear & Emotional Vulnerability
↓ Why #3: Media Influence & Low Scientific Awareness
↓ Why #4: Social Reinforcement
🎯 Root Cause: No culturally-sensitive platforms exist that distinguish legitimate practices from exploitation, explain psychological mechanisms accessibly, provide integrated support pathways, and create safe spaces for the "gray zone" of uncertainty.

How Might We...?

HMW #1: Provide 24/7 confidential counseling with grounding and culturally-sensitive support for students in distress?
HMW #2: Help users distinguish between legitimate cultural practitioners and those exploiting supernatural beliefs?
HMW #3: Use AI-based assessments to guide users toward suitable medical, cultural, or integrated support?
HMW #4: Build a transparent certification system for ethical cultural practitioners who respect mental health boundaries?

Court Cases Analysis

Key Legal Precedents

  • P.Rajagopal vs State of Tamil Nadu (2019): Case involving alleged witchcraft as cause of being in love.
  • Ammini vs State of Kerala (1997): Family attempted to ruin another with black magic practices.
  • Sangeet vs State of Haryana (2012): Highlights violence stemming from supernatural beliefs.

Conclusion

Key Learnings

  • • Young adults occupy a "gray zone" between scientific education and cultural tradition
  • • Integrated support systems are needed that honor both realities
  • • Technology can bridge belief and reason when designed with cultural sensitivity
  • • Safety, ethics, and privacy must be designed in from the start
